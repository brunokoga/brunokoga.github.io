Biking from Berlin to Copenhagen 🚲

April 12, 2025

Copenhaaaaaagen

April 21, 2025

Last day starting now! ⏱️ We left our lovely cabin behind and started cycling under a grey sky. We were set for an 80km day, so not the shortest of days! First quick stop: UNESCO world heritage Stevns Klint. Being an early bird has its perks, as we were almost the only ones there.

Then we powered through 43km until Køge, where we had a poké bowl (we were getting a little bit tired of bread with cheese being the only vegetarian option) and stocked up snacks at the supermarket. One of them being a peanut butter mochi??!!! 🤩

Shortly before arriving in our final destination of this whole trip, we stopped at the beach for some rest 🫠 Boy, it was hard to get up on the bike for the last 12km after that pause 😮‍💨

Happy and exhausted we finally arrived at our cool hostel! Two weeks ago, we had sent ourselves a package to the hostel, containing more clothes, socks, underwear and bagpacks for our stuff. Unfortunately the package hasn’t arrived and we went only dressed in our rain stuff (🥶) to the laundromat once more. Just a typical day in the city! (See Rostock)

Until now, we’ve been enjoying Copenhagens deer park, it’s lovely streets and buildings, the cute cafes and a cool Lars von Trier exhibition in an old church. One more day, one more cinnamon roll to go!

Rødvig

April 20, 2025

It was a rainy night and a rainy morning. We had 80km in front of us today, but we didn’t start cycling until the rain stopped, around 11. One hour and 20km later, we had our Easter breakfast at the same café we’ve been two days ago. My respect for Danish bread, butter and cheese 🍞🧈🧀 is growing day by day. Maike also had a cheesecake and a kanelsnurrer (which are danish cinnamon rolls on steroids).

The promise was that the sun would shine a little bit during the afternoon, but that, unfortunately, didn’t happen. At least we had a lot of wind on our butts 🌬️🍑 and the kilometers passed by a little bit faster than usual. After 45km, we had more coffee, bread, butter and cheese for lunch in Præstrø (good luck saying that!). The grey skies and cold weather didn’t make it not so interesting for us to explore the towns along the way or even take breaks so we kept pushing the last 40km and arrived at our camping place at 5:30. The reception was closed but there was a leaflet with Maike’s name on it, containing the rules and general information about the camping. There was also key 🔑 (🤔) and instructions to get to or tent spot. And, yes 🙌! We had a cabin! Since we’re the only people camping with a tent here (and that has been true for almost all our camping places), the owners decided to offer us the cabin because why not? We take that!

Instead of pitching the tent, we had a hot shower before biking to an Asian all-you-can-eat restaurant. Back to our cabin, we are cozily warm, have wi-fi and can charge our phones. Not bad for our last night before arriving in Copenhagen 🤩!

Visiting Stege & Møns Klint

April 19, 2025

Waking up to rain we decided to have a cosy breakfast inside the tent ⛺️ Not so cosy was the fact that the rain kept falling and was not about to stop anytime soon. That meant biking to Møns Klint would not be possible.

Buuuuuut luck was on our side (I still think it’s because of the four leaf clover I found hehe) because Møn happens to have a free bus that goes around the island all day long. So we hopped on the bus 🚌 and went to Stege, where we had an amazing coffee and one of the best breads (sorry Papa) we ever ate. Mine was with entire chocolate pieces instead of chocolate spread - is this typical danish? 🇩🇰

Warm, dry and happy we continued to Møns Klint, which turned out to be more touristic than anticipated but nonetheless absolutely beautiful. See for yourself 🤩

Hårbølle on Møn

April 18, 2025

Yesterday we left the official path and detoured to the motel because the ferry that would have brought us to Bogø and then to Møn is only running as of May 1st. Happens, when you’re so early in the season! So instead of a ferry, we went via the very trustworthy-looking, not-rusty-at-all Storstrømsbroen bridge. From the bridge we had a beautiful view on the new Storstrømsbroen bridge that is being build to replace this one in the coming year. With this in mind and heavy wind gusts from the side, I couldn’t bike fast enough to be on land again haha 😮‍💨

After an uneventful 60km (with very delicious coffee stops though) we arrived on the Island of Møn, in Hårbølle, at our most beautiful camp site yet: we’ve pitched the tent between the dunes, right next to the ocean. Lovely! 🌅

Guldborgsund

April 17, 2025

Staying true to our promise of yesterday, Bruno’s (crazy) circadian rhythm woke us up at 05:45, just in time for sunrise! Our temporary rooftop terrace is facing east - meaning we had first class seats for the spectacle 🌅

After breakfast outside, we went to the port of Rostock to catch the 9am ferry to Gedser. Together with just a few other cyclists, a Swedish school class and a Japanese German chain-smoking ultra stylish (no irony) rockstar group, we boarded the ship and arrived 2 hours later in Denmark 🇩🇰 When we finally exited from the ‚belly‘ of the ferry it was as if someone has turned the winds up and the temperatures down 🥲 I would have preferred it the other way round but oh well 🤷‍♀️ At least it’s sunny! And we’re not here for leisure anyways hehe 😛

With a quick Falafel-Dürüm pit stop in a sleepy but cute town called Nykøbing Falster we detoured to an autoroute Motel in the middle of nowhere, totaling around 58km for the day. Part of the motel is a renowned and exquisite restaurant, that we entered, feeling a little misplaced in our outdoor clothes (at least we’re showered 😮‍💨) but nonetheless we enjoyed the food. Afterwards - reading and catching up on sleep!

Day 5: Güstrow

April 16, 2025

During the night it rained but in the morning it was already sunny again 🌅 We started our day slowly with a big breakfast and coffee at the tranquil Müritz lake. The goal for today was Güstrow, in a distance of 79 kilometres from us. Let’s go!

During our second breakfast break I found a four leaf clover for the first time ever 🍀We’re off to a good start I guess 🤩

Some cobble stone roads and hills later, we stopped again in Krakow am See for ice cream before arriving quite early at the camping spot for the night. The four leaf clover charm worked: the camping place had an all-you-can eat buffet for dinner and breakfast tonight! Perfect for two hungry bikers hehe 😋

Rostock

April 16, 2025

We had our warmest night so far (around 12 degrees) and woke up early on our weird-kind-of-in-the-hotels’s-parking-lot camping spot to enjoy the buffet breakfast from the hotel. Fueled with scrambled eggs, lattes, fresh fruits and local honey we started cycling today’s 60km a bit before 10am. Our plan was to take advantage of the “short” distance and have lunch in our destination, Rostock. With that in mind we kept our rhythm through the warm and sunny day and made only two quick stops to rest and snack.

Once we arrived in town, we quickly stopped at the outdoor store to buy gas and a few camping supplies that we were missing and went straight to have burgers and beers followed by cake and coffee.

With our bellies full, we went to the flat that we’re staying tonight but there was no time to rest. After a quick shower we went straight to do our town errands: groceries and laundry. With clean clothes and a full food bag again, we finally had an hour or two to enjoy in town.

We witnessed a beautiful sunset, with an astonishing purplishly pink sky, even though our view was a little bit blocked by the city buildings. This inspired me and Maike to make an agreement that we would wake up extra early tomorrow at 6am to watch the sunrise from the terrace of our flat and have breakfast before taking the ferry at 9am. I’m not so sure that this will happen but let’s see…

Waren (Müritz)

April 14, 2025

The day started with a challenging off-road stretch through the forest. If I didn’t know that it’s spring , I’d mistake it for fall 🍁🍂

Speaking of spring - the weather gods are on our side and bathe us in sunshine and a gezellig warm 18 degrees !

After a second breakfast in Wesenberg, where we got some good ol’ Remouladenbrötchen, it was time to hit the pedals because we had 76km ahead of us! 76km full of lush green teletubby-like hills, perky clouds, endless fields and blossoming magnolia and cherry blossom trees 🌲 Mecklenburgische Seenplatte, you’re stunning! Your beauty almost makes up for the burning leg muscles and more that your hills have caused us 😝

Upon arrival at our final destination Waren, we turned into two hungry vegetarians that were stranded in a city full of meat and fish restaurants. So we ended up at - surprise - an Italian restaurant again! Gnocchi the third, this time was the best 🏆

Now, we’re camped at the Müritz lake with nice, chatty neighbours who let us use their electricity to write some Polarsteps 😎 Bonne nuit!

Wagnitzsee

April 13, 2025

To our surprise neither of us had a muscle ache or any pain today 🥹 You’d think that after doing 90km out of the blue your body needs to recover but now I know that all the spinning classes were worth it 🤑 And maybe waking up by the sun 🌞 rising in your face is helping as well!

So we swung our not-so-virgin butts on our bikes again and took off for a 68km ride. Short and sweet, no? Again, the holy banana bread was devoured by us by (more by me as you can see). Thank you Mete and Bruno for baking two breads!

We had another Italian lunch (hello again, Gnocchi) and eventually camped at a nice lake. Also, we realized that it’s quite hard to take pictures/videos while biking .. we’ll make sure to stop now and then to capture the spring blossoming landscape of Mecklenburg Vorpommern tomorrow! 📸

Day 2: Berlin to Zehdenick

April 12, 2025

With a tiny delay, we left with our home and beloved WG member Mete behind and saddled up on our rental gravel bikes 🚲

The official start of the route is the Brandenburger Tor, that’s where we had to take a picture, of course 🤓

The first stretch was already going to be the longest one of the whole trip: 90km! Can our virgin butts and legs handle this? 😵‍💫😵‍💫

Yes, they could! Fuelled by selfmade banana bread, ice cream and a big pasta lunch break the kilometers were flying by. Leaving sunny Hennigsdorf and Oranienburg behind, we rode past a last beautiful stretch along the Havel before arriving at our final destination: a camp site next to a historic water tower in Zehdenick. While next to us, people are raving in the tower (special event tonight 🪩) we’re laying in our tent with no roof, trying to see the stars ✨ The only thing we see though are the party lights and the full moon 🌕 Still magical!
