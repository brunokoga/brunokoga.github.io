Last post and next steps

May 8, 2023

Better (one week) late than never:

For the past 50 days, Polarsteps was part of every single one of our evenings. Sometimes 🙂, sometimes 🙃. Not the app in particular but the creation of texts: reflecting on our day, trying to wrap up our experiences in words, finding the right angle. It became our diary and the digital, shared memory of our hike.

To not forget - that was our motivation.

But even more motivating than merely wanting to remember, was knowing that you are reading! To hope that you are maybe maybe smiling at our texts and pictures, that you like and enjoy these memories as much as we did 🥰

To everyone who sent us their kindness through likes, comments or private messages; to everyone who in some way or another followed along our crazy journey of 88 temples - thank you, obrigado and dankeeeee 💜🧡

Now that one week has passed, all the pain and exhaustion is forgotten and we already try to walk as much as we can again 🤪 Which is working well because from now on we’ll be visiting the big cities in Japan 🇯🇵 🏙️ We’ll create another Polarsteps Trip soon!

Temple 86-88

May 6, 2023

Today was the day! Now we can proudly say we have seen it all 😎 ‘It’ in our case stands for the 88 temples of Shikoku island 🏯📿🎋

How did it go, the day we’ve been waiting for so long?

Overall it was a pretty slow day. I guess we felt a little lazy or ‘temple-tired’ 😴 It’s a thing! After having visited Nr. 86 and Nr. 87 it was time for our certificate (we didn’t even officially reach the end but who cares 🤭) Before the pilgrims start their final climb to the last one, temple 88, they can collect their completion certificate at a museum at the bottom of the hill. There, they had a cool 3D map of the pilgrimage and some interesting statistics 📊

Two certificates, a picture and a couple of snacks later we continued our journey. Because we have been warned last week by another pilgrim that the last climb would be very difficult, even dangerous, we decided to take the safe but longer route along the road 🚗 When we finally arrived we were lucky to catch Natsuko and two other Brazilians still! They had to run for the bus (again!) but of course Bruno managed a quick selfie with almost everybody 🤳 We learned from them that the climb wasn’t so hard after all!

Now, one might think our journey is over … but we’ve decided to come full circle ⭕️ and continue to temple 1, where it all started. So two more days of hiking and then you’re finally getting rid of us! 🥹

Maike & Bruno 👣

Temple 71-76

May 2, 2023

Today was our last “easy day” (less than 20km) from the pilgrimage. Also, we broke the 1000km mark since we started! More and more we’re having the feeling that we’re almost there 😬!

We visited temples 71 to 76 and the highlight of the day was definitely temple 75. This temple is so important that the whole city is named after the temple (Zentsūji). The reason for that is because Zentsūji is the birthplace of Kūkai, which is this guy who is accredited with founding the Shikoku pilgrimage. And since this year would be his 1250th birthday, there were some celebrations happening there. It was by far the busiest temple we visited until now (also because it’s Golden Week here in Japan, a big holiday week).

Temple 66-67

April 30, 2023

Since today we would start the day with a Henro Korogashi (the difficult passages of the Shikoku 88 temples pilgrimage), we had breakfast at 6am and soon after we began our ascent to temple 66, the highest temple of the whole pilgrimage. Two hours later we arrived at the temple (which is located at an elevation of 911 meters) and thankfully we had obentos 🍙 prepared by our 94 year old host (and packed in a newspaper 🗞️😅), since there was no other way of getting food up there.

We had no surprises for the rest of the day. From temple 66 we walked down for about two hours more and visited temple 67 and yet again another two hours stretch to our accommodation.

Temple 46-51

April 25, 2023

The forecast for today was rain, rain and more rain. So, after having a late breakfast at the hotel (7 AM), we had no choice other than start our walk at 7:40 under a light drizzle. It was probably the coldest morning since we started our pilgrimage (7°) and we knew that it would be a long day (6 temples over 32 km).

We arrived at our hostel after 5 PM completely exhausted and besides visiting 6 temples (which is our record for number of temples visited in one single day), the other highlights of the day were: a beautiful mist over the forest 🌫️; cute notes by children giving us the much appreciated support to keep going 💪; Maike eating peanut butter from the jar like a true hiker should do; and an absolutely delicious probiotic vegan dinner here in Matsuyama (where we will be staying for two days).

Temple 44-45

April 24, 2023

Today we had a “loop day”: we started and finished the day at the same spot, meaning that we could leave our backpack at the hotel and walk light. Even though we walked for 26km, it feels almost like a rest day.

The weather was milder than the past days and we hiked most of the time on unpaved terrain. Also, the two temples that we visited today were astonishing. PS: Temple 45 was really spectacular. We arrived through a dense forest, with massive trees 🌲. On its approach we passed by 36 Buddhas statues 🗿 and a locked gate that gives access to a challenging (and apparently dangerous) climb 🧗‍♀️ on the big rocks 🪨 that surround the temple (you have to request the key at the temple but we preferred not to risk our lives this time 😅). Is it breakfast or dinner

April 23, 2023

Breakfast in Japan is so different than the one I usually eat at home. Here, I have already eaten more fish for breakfast than in my entire life before (I actually only ate salmon really before becoming vegetarian) 🐟

There is always rice and miso soup (misoshiro), green tea and salted ‘Nori’ to make your own mini-sushi. Pickles and something like scrambled eggs but much much better called ‘Tamagoyaki’ cannot be missed either! The exact same food can be found at the dinner table too, just with more variations of fish. And tempura 🍤😋 But to be honest, a lot of times I don’t even know what I’m eating exactly 🤦‍♀️ So, it’s up to you now to decide which one of those two pictures is breakfast or dinner !?

At 7:30, after having this delicious breakfast this morning (the best one so far) we accidentally went for a 20km sprint and already arrived at 12:30 at our accommodation for the night! Oops, I guess this means we had another chill day of reading, napping and eating 🤭

Temple 41-43

April 20, 2023

The morning started how we liked: waking up without alarm (at 5:30 😴), having a delicious breakfast (musli, yoghurt, blueberry 🫐, kiwi 🥝) and filtered coffee with oat milk (!) ☕️. We left our beautiful accommodation at 7 on the dot.

On our first temple of the day (41) we had a delightful surprise and met our friend Natsuko (the lovely Japanese lady who we met on our first days of pilgrimage!). We walked with her (and her friend) for the rest of the day.

We even went through a nice path where a few years ago a typhoon 🌪️ caused a flood and made the trail impassable. Apparently this year is the first year since that part of the trail is open again. We had to go over (and under) lots of trees and branches that had been knocked over by either the tornado or flood.

Even though we haven’t visited half of the temples yet (almost there!), today we realized that we are past the half of our pilgrimage in kilometers: we have walked more than 650km so far!

PS: Natsuko is really fascinated by the way we eat carrots. Every time she sees us eating a whole carrot 🥕 she laughs and takes a picture 😂📸

What our days look like

April 19, 2023

Even though every day is unique and special on its own (especially when reflecting upon it in the evenings), the days all together feel more or less the same. They become this one big blur 🌀Sounds paradoxical but … do you know what I mean?

To demonstrate, I can explain to you our rhythm of a ‘normal’ day:

5am - 6am 🕕

The alarm rings at 5 but most of the time I don’t hear it and Bruno is already awake half an hour earlier anyways 🙃 We always always have breakfast otherwise we can’t leave the house 😜 Of course we eat good ol‘ Müsli, fruits and …

🙌peanutbutter🙌. After stretching we start our hike at 6.

10am 🕙

Time for a second breakfast! Best kind of breakfast hehe 🫠 If we’re lucky we find a convenience store around that time. There you can find all kinds of food and we buy some 🍙 and 🥕 and eat it in the parking lot (see picture). That’s the life! 😎

Until 3/4pm 🕓

Walking, walking, talking, eating, singing, walking, arriving! After the check-in at each place we are called via the room telephone to our showers. After shower we chill for a bit in a very hot tub called ofuro ♨️ It’s good for the sore muscles (and stretching again helps a lot too) 💪

6pm 🕕

Dinner time! We either buy some Bento 🍱 boxes at the supermarket, eat at a restaurant or if we have a kitchen we cook 👩‍🍳 Most of the times we’re lazy and … cook 😝

9pm 🕘

Hop in the kimono, quickly write a loooong Polarsteps post and then it’s time to sleep 😴 9pm is the new midnight here! With that said: Gute Nacht 😘

Maike 🐰

Oh happy day 🎶

April 16, 2023

When you do a long pilgrimage like the Shikoku Henro, there are always difficult days: days where your feet hurt, days where the rain never stops and you get soaking wet, days where you can’t find good food and you feel bad for eating so much crap. There are days where your accommodation sucks, days where your intestines don’t work like they should, days where you get furious because you overpay for things that you regret. There are days where you think “why the hell am I doing this?”.

And then, to balance it out, there are days like today 🤩!

We woke up with a beautiful view from our window, watching the sun rising in the ocean and had a tasty breakfast prepared by our 78 years old host. The day was beautiful and sunny, but not too hot. We quickly found public toilets for the two or three times that either me or Maike needed to pee. We met David again along the way and had a very nice chat. We found a beach and went for a quick dip in the sea. We had the best coffee we had so far in Japan and received delicious cookies as osettai from the lovely coffee shop owner. We had a mouth-watering Okonomiyaki for dinner which was cooked right in front of us. And even though we hiked for more than 26km, we could barely feel any tiredness at the end of the day.

There are these days where you just cannot complain about life 🤍🥰

Bruno 🍪

400km done ✅

April 14, 2023

Although we didn’t visit any temples, today we had a few accomplishments: it was the first day that we walked more than 30km after we took our rest days (36km to be more precise 😮‍💨). Our accommodation for tonight is a whole house 🏠! We booked only a room for two (like we always do) but when we arrived here we were told that they only rent the place for one group at a time. This means that we don’t have to pay anything extra but we have a 3-bedroom house entirely for us. Not bad 😎!

And finally but not least, we received a pack of strawberries 🍓 as an osettai today from a lady that was driving past us and stopped to give us a “presentto”. We’re feeling that we are the happiest and luckiest pilgrims today ✌️🍀.

Temple 36

The day started with a quick stop at the convenience store to buy food for the day, since we knew we would have a stretch of ~20km without shops today. Right after that we had a short hike to temple 36 and decided to take a shortcut to continue the day…

No temples, lots of good food

Today we had s short day (“only” 23km) and visited no temples. On our first break in the morning we met Natsuko again (a kind and smiley japanese lady who we’ve been meeting almost everyday since day 3!). After that we were starving and went to a restaurant next door from our accommodation. The menu was in japanese, the waitress spoke only japanese and our solution was to ask the cook to bring us whatever he felt like cooking. The result? We had our best meal in Japan so far! We had Takenoko (bamboo shoots), konbu (sea weed), daikon (radish), karaage (deep fried chicken), chahan (fried rice), katsuo no tataki (broiled skipjack tuna sashimi) and lots of other things that we don’t even know the name. Everything was local, fresh and absolutely delicious!

Temple 23

March 31, 2023

We woke up at 6 and visited temple 23 (薬王寺) which was only 200m distant from our accommodation. The temple had a beautiful view to the bay and we could enjoy a beautiful sunrise. After that we came back and our lovely host cooked delicious oniguiris 🍙 (my favorite food from my childhood!) and tamagoyaki 🥚 for our breakfast and gave us more oniguiris and chocolate 🍫 as an osettai (gifts given to pilgrims of the Shikoku Pilgrimage) for us to have lunch on the way. It’s amazing how ours hosts have been spoiling us since the very beginning of this pilgrimage 👶🏻

We had three choices of paths for today: the shortest (of 20k), a “scenic” path (25k) and a “difficult and hilly but with astonishing views” path (4.5h through the mountains plus 15k). Of course we chose the easiest one 😌. We arrived at our accommodation (an old building with a sea view 🌊) at 2pm. We finally had some time to chill and do laundry 🧺 (two luxuries for a pilgrim 🙃).

Temple 22

March 30, 2023

Today we had our earliest start so far. At 6:00 sharp 🕕 our host drove us to the same spot where we finished our day yesterday, right after temple 21 (太龍寺). After a “quick” walk (about 10k) we arrived at temple 22 (平等寺), which was the only temple we visited today. While we were having breakfast there we saw two big groups of elderly japanese pilgrims which were all very polite and wished us a good day, one by one. We probably replied a hundred “Ohayoo gozaimasu” back to them.

On our way to our next accommodation (more than 20km walking distance), we were stopped twice in two different occasions by two japanese men wanting to help us showing a “better path” to the next temple. Both of them carefully explained an alternative route through the mountains instead of the normal route (which is on the road, through tunnels). They said that on the top of the mountain we would have a very nice view from the ocean 🌊 and it would be worth the extra effort.

They were absolutely right! After hiking up this alternative route, we were presented to an astonishing view of the ocean! There was even a swing there! It’s not everyday that you find yourself on a swing on the top of the mountain with an ocean view 😊! And we couldn’t have chosen a better day to see the ocean for the first time during this pilgrimage: the day was gorgeous, sunny and warm. On the top of the mountain we also met and had a nice chat with a japanese girl (Maya) who was walking the Henro with her mother. She lives in Shikoku and invited us to visit her after we finish our pilgrimage!

The last kilometers along the coast were a bit exhausting (today we walked a total of 32km), but we arrived at our accommodation right before 5pm, which means that we had more than 10 hours hiking today!

Bruno 🐒

Temple 6-10

March 25, 2023

After having a japanese style breakfast (with rice 🍚 , misoshiro 🥣 and vegetables) we started our second day with a short walk to temple number 6, Anrakuji (安楽寺) and right after to temple number 7 Jūrakuji (十楽寺), where we met Flynn, a fellow pilgrim from Australia. The first of the two highlights of the day was temple 8, Kumadaniji (熊谷寺), which was surrounded by beautiful trees 🌳 and even-more-beautiful Sakura trees. Temple 9 Hōrinji (法輪寺) followed. Since it was already past noon, we were all starving on our way to the next temple and tried to stop by a bakery that Google Maps pointed us. There was no bakery to be seen but, while asking a random local for directions, we were surprised by our second and most exciting highlight of today: we were invited to his family home 🏡 where we had the chance to meet a lovely couple and their three boys (14, 12 and 9 years old). They offered us a ride 🚙 (which we politely refused) and plenty of fresh water 💦. While we were having a chat with them at their home, they told us that today was the husband’s mom’s birthday: she was turning ninety years old! For our surprise, she showed up 👵🏻 and we could wish her happy birthday 🥳🎈 in person! We left their home with a smile on each one of our faces and headed to our final temple of the day. Temple 1-5

March 24, 2023

We woke up at 7am, had a quick breakfast 🥪 at the hostel and headed to Tokushima train station 🚉, where we took a train to Bandō station to finally start our pilgrimage 🥾!

After leaving the station, a short walk led us to the first temple, Ryōzenji (霊山寺).

We were expecting to see a lot of other pilgrims, but the temples and the path were surprisingly empty. We saw in total maybe 10 other pilgrims. This is also the time of the year where the Sakura (cherry blossom) trees are blooming and we could see a lot of these beautiful flowers along the way 🌸. It seems like we couldn’t have chosen a better time to start our walk!

Today we visited four other temples: Gokurakuji (極楽寺), Dainichiji (大日時), Anrakuji (安楽寺) and Jozōji (地蔵寺).

Tonight we’re staying in a japanese-style accommodation, sleeping in futons instead of beds. After soaking ourselves in a nice and warm ofuro 🧖‍♀️, we’ll have the our first experience eating a japanese-style dinner 🍱 (and breakfast tomorrow).
